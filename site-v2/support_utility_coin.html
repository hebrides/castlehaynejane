Farm Utility Token

A Coin Rooted in Real Land

Not speculation. A token that gives you perpetual access to a working farm on the Cape Fear River.

The Concept

Ownership you can actually use.

The Castle Hayne Jane utility coin isn't an investment vehicle — it's an access token. Holders receive standing access to the farm: camping rights, event priority, harvest shares, and a voice in how common spaces develop. Think of it less like a stock and more like a lifetime membership tied to a specific piece of land.

Unlike most crypto projects, every coin maps to something concrete: 26 acres on the Northeast Cape Fear River, a peach orchard being planted now, a pole barn under construction, and a growing calendar of events. The land exists. The work is happening. The token formalizes your connection to it.

We're in the early design phase for the coin structure. If you're interested in being part of the initial holder cohort, get on the list below.

Token Utility

What holding the coin means.

Benefits are tiered by the number of coins held. All tiers include the baseline access package.

Baseline Access (1+ coins)

  • Perpetual camping rights on designated farm sites
  • Priority registration for all farm events
  • Annual harvest share invitation
  • Token-holder newsletter with farm updates

Extended Access (10+ coins)

  • All baseline benefits
  • One complimentary weekend farm rental per year
  • Named acknowledgment in the farm's permanent record
  • Input on future common-space development
  • Invitation to annual holder gathering on the farm
Structure

Designed to stay grounded.

We're building the coin structure with simplicity and longevity in mind. Fixed supply. No yield farming. No staking mechanics. The coin's value comes from what it unlocks on real land — not from market dynamics designed to enrich early buyers at later buyers' expense.

The initial issuance will be offered to the farm's existing supporter community first, before any public sale. Details on the smart contract, issuance schedule, and wallet requirements will be published here as they're finalized.

We're partnering with legal and technical advisors to make sure this is done properly. If you have relevant expertise and want to be involved, reach out through the contact page.
